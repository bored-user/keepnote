Refleksive possessiver

These are used when the object belongs to the subject.

In English, one can say “he loves his book” (which can mean that a guy loves a book he owns or loves a book that some other guy owns) and “he loves his own book” (this can only mean that the guy loves a book he owns).

 

In Norwegian, however, “han elsker hans bok” (and “han elsker boken hans”, for that matter) only means that a guy loves a book owned by another guy. If one wants to say that someone does something to a thing they own, the correct form is using the refleksive possessive form, like so: “han elsker sin bok”/ “han elsker boken sin” ("he loves his [own] book").
