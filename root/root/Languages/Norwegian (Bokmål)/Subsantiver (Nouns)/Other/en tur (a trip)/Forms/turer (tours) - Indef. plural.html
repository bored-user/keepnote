Indef. plural

The indefinite plural form convention is the way of pluralizing indefinite nouns. It basically consist in adding the letters “r” (in most cases: if the noun ends in a vowel) or “er” (in most cases:  if they end in a consonant other than “r”) at the end of the noun (unless they're neutral- which doesn't add any letters).

For instance: “katt” means “cat”, while “katter” means “cats”. There are, however, irregular plural nouns, like “mann” ("man"), which becomes “menn” ("men"), instead of “manner” (which would be the regular form).
