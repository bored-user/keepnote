Posessive pronouns

There are basically two ways of expressing possession in Norwegian: using the definite or indefiniteforms.

When using the definite form, the noun is converted to its definite form and the article is written after it. This emphasizes the object and it's considered less formal.

Examples:

  • faren min (my father)
  • mora mi (my mother)
  • hunden min (my dog)
  • barnet mitt (my child)
  • foreldrene mine (my parents)

When using the indefinite form, the noun is converted to its indefinite form and the article is written before it. This emphasizes the article (person who possesses) and it's considered more formal.

Examples:

  • min far (my father)
  • min mor (my mother)
  • min hund (my dog)
  • mitt barn (my child)
  • mine foreldre (my parents)
